# Residual Probe Interpretation Gate

The residual correctness-probe direction does not appear meaningfully sparse in the Goodfire layer-19 SAE basis relative to random residual directions. Under decoder-cosine alignment, the top 50 SAE features capture 1.96% of alignment L2 mass versus a random-vector mean of 1.36% and random 95th percentile of 1.46%; under encoder projection, the top 50 capture 2.18% versus a random-vector mean of 1.93% and random 95th percentile of 2.08%. Gini is also close to the null distribution in both views: 0.604 versus 0.594 for decoder-cosine and 0.603 versus 0.602 for encoder projection. This clears the "some top features exist" bar, but not the stronger "small interpretable feature set explains the probe" bar. Per the preregistered gate, detailed feature characterization is skipped; the result to report is that the residual probe's correctness signal is present but distributed across the residual stream rather than cleanly localized to a compact set of SAE features.
